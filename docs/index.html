1. Self-Regulation

Focuses attention, controls impulses, and adapts behavior to meet challenges, enabling goal-directed actions despite pressure or distractions.

2. Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent synthesis by integrating metacognitive data and allocentric perspectives, giving meaning to experience

Note:

  • It requires openness to new perspectives and a willingness to adjust preconception

2a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates competing claims by independently assessing the probability (of the claim) and reliability (of the source), then comparing those assessments against logical integrity and material relevance.

2b. Objectivity

Identifies susceptibility to bias and applies empirical and rational standards uniformly when assessing the validity and consistency of claims.

Example

  • Applies the “Veil of ignorance”
  • Prevents desire, identity, or attachment from overriding standards

2c. Fairness

Applies objective balance between empathy and obligation across reciprocal interactions, irrespective of conditions.

Example

  • Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and avoid hypocrisy

2d. Self-Reflection

Analyzes past mistakes to identify patterns, then formulates if/then strategies for similar future situations. Engages in constructive reflection rather than rumination.

2e. Self-Efficacy

Emerges when confidence is grounded in an accurate assessment of one’s proficiency, but it wanes if skills are unused or untested

Examples

  • Recognizes one’s skills and achievements and integrates them into one’s sense of self
  • Treats setbacks as a lack of skill, not as lack of self-worth

3. Self-Discipline

Consistent application of self-regulation to align behavior with end-goals and principles, while avoiding inflexibility and complacency

Example

  • Adjusts interim goals as needed to stay consistent with long-term goals

3a. Perseverance

Sustains effort and endurance amid hardship or challenge, countering fear-based avoidance by commiting to a goal.

Examples

  • Treats fear-response as a skill to refine
  • Confronts fear gradually to build resiliency

3b. Temperance

Manages willpower as a limited resource, without drifting into comfortable neglect or slack

Examples

  • Allocates 80% of one’s effort to one’s most valued priorities
  • Summarizes emotions into one word to stay focused
