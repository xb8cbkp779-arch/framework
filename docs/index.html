1. Self-Regulation

Refocus attention, controls impulses, and adapts behavior to meet demands, sustaining goal-directed actions despite pressure or distractions.

2. Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent synthesis by integrating metacognitive data and allocentric perspectives, resulting in enduring rationales that stand the test of time

2a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates competing claims by attributing probability and reliability to each and then comparing those attributions by their logical integrity and contextual significance.

Examples:

  • Considers new perspectives with a willingness to adjust preconceptions
  • Chooses truth over self-justification and self-serving logic

2b. Objectivity

Identifies susceptibility to bias and applies empirical and rational standards uniformly when assessing the validity and consistency of claims.

Example

  • Applies the “Veil of ignorance”
  • Prevents desire, identity, or attachment from overriding standards

2c. Fairness

Applies objective balance between empathety and accountable obligation across reciprocal interactions, irrespective of conditions.

Examples

  • Avoids both prejudice and favoritism
  • Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and avoid hypocrisy

2d. Self-Reflection

Turns lived experiences into lasting insights; avoiding rumination, self-blame, or decision paralysis.

Example

  • Reflects on past mistakes to create if/then decisions, applying them to similar situations in the future.

2e. Self-Efficacy

Emerges when confidence is grounded in an accurate assessment of one’s proficiency, but wanes if skills are unused or untested

Examples

  • Recognizes one’s skills and achievements and integrates them into one’s sense of self
  • Treats setbacks as a lack of skill, not as lack of self-worth

3. Self-Discipline

Consistent application of self-regulation to align behavior with end-goals and principles, while avoiding rigidity or complacency

Example

  • Adjusts interim goals as needed to stay consistent with long-term goals

3a. Perseverance

Sustains effort and resolve amid hardship or challenge, countering fear-based avoidance by committing to an end goal.

Examples

  • Treats fear-response as a skill to refine
  • Confronts fear gradually and recurrently

3b. Temperance

Manages willpower as a limited resource, without drifting into comfortable slack

Examples

  • Allocates 80% of one’s effort to one’s most valued priorities
  • Summarizes emotions into one word in order to stay focused
