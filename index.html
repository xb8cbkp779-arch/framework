Self-Regulation
and Biases

0. SELF-REGULATION

Sustains goal-directed behavior by (a) inhibiting impulsive responses, (b) directing and sustaining attention, and (c) regulating emotion under pressure, distraction, or uncertainty.

1. Sense-Making

Constructs a coherent working model of reality by (a) integrating metacognitive signals (confidence, bias cues, emotional arousal), (b) incorporating allocentric perspectives (outside-view, other agents’ models, base rates), (c) reconciling conflicts across inputs, and (d) revising preconceptions when warranted.

1a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates an assertion by (a) estimating the plausibility of its proposition, (b) assessing source reliability, and (c) stress-testing justification for logical validity and evidential relevance.

Behavioral markers: (a) distinguishes claim vs evidence, (b) seeks base rates, (c) attempts falsification, (d) tracks uncertainty explicitly.

  • Doesn’t try to disprove
  • Overlooks general statistical trends
  • Hesitates to consider differing perspectives
  • Mixes up what sounds true with what’s logically sound
  • Overlooks uncertainty
  • Disregards odds when emotions run high

1b. Objectivity

Identifies uneven standards in belief formation, then applies (a) empirical criteria (measurement, replication, base rates) and (b) rational criteria (veil of ignorance, non-contradiction, Bayes-consistent updating) uniformly across favored and disfavored conclusions.

  • Uses uneven rules for different cases
  • Sees things in a way that fits personal views
  • Clings to existing beliefs
  • Assumes others think the same way

1c. Fairness

Applies impartial balance between (a) empathy (context, constraints, intent) and (b) accountability norms (reciprocity, consequences, repair) in reciprocal interactions, irrespective of status, convenience, or mood.

Example: Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and avoid hypocrisy.
  • Reduces ability to empathize with others
  • Blocks situational factors and blames personal traits.
  • Excuses self, blames others
  • Judges morally instead of contextually

1d. Self-Reflection

Analyzes past outcomes to (a) detect recurring patterns, (b) identify controllable levers, and (c) convert insight into implementation rules, avoiding rumination by translating reflection into testable action.

Format: “If X cue occurs, then I will do Y behavior within Z time.”
  • attributes success to oneself and failure to external factors.
  • Judges based on results, not the process
  • Distorts how we revise our past views

1e. Self-Efficacy

Maintains confidence calibrated to evidence by (a) anchoring self-belief in demonstrated competencies, (b) interpreting setbacks as skill gaps or strategy mismatches rather than global self-worth deficits, and (c) preventing skill atrophy through periodic re-testing.

Examples: (a) integrates achievements into self-concept, (b) treats setbacks as training data.
  • Overrates personal ability
  • Doubts personal accomplishments are real
  • Focuses too much on failures

2. Self-Discipline

Applies self-regulation consistently to align behavior with long-term goals and principles by (a) selecting priorities, (b) sustaining execution, and (c) preventing drift into rigidity or complacency.

2a. Perseverance

Sustains effort amid hardship by (a) countering avoidance impulses, (b) reaffirming commitment to the target outcome, and (c) using exposure and skill-building to reduce fear-driven withdrawal over time.

Examples: (a) treats fear responses as trainable signals, (b) uses graduated exposure to build resilience.
  • Panics about failing
  • Avoids hard things out of fear
  • Ignores long-term benefits

2b. Temperance

Allocates willpower as a limited resource by (a) ranking demands by importance, (b) reducing decision load and temptation bandwidth, and (c) choosing “good-enough” regulation strategies that preserve capacity for what matters most.

Example: Compresses emotions into a single label to regain focus.
  • Makes self-control fade over time
  • Tires out the mind with too many options
  • Stays stuck due to what’s already invested
