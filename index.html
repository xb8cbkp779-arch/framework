Self-Regulation

0. SELF-REGULATION

Sustains goal-directed behavior by (a) inhibiting impulsive responses, (b) directing and sustaining attention, and (c) regulating emotion under pressure, distraction, or uncertainty.

1. Sense-Making

Assembles a coherent working model of reality by (a) integrating metacognitive signals (confidence, bias cues, emotional arousal), (b) incorporating allocentric perspectives (other parties' models, base rates), (c) reconciling conflicts across inputs, and (d) revising preconceptions when warranted.

1a. Critical Thinking

Evaluates an assertion by (a) estimating the plausibility of its proposition, (b) assessing source reliability, (c) distinguishing claim, evidence, and inference as distinct justificatory elements, and (d) evaluating logical validity and evidential relevance.

Behavioral markers: (a) distinguishes claim vs evidence, (b) seeks base rates, (c) attempts falsification, (d) tracks uncertainty explicitly.

1b. Objectivity

Identifies uneven standards in belief formation, then applies (a) empirical criteria (measurement, replication, base rates) and (b) rational criteria (veil of ignorance, non-contradiction, Bayes-consistent updating) uniformly across favored and disfavored conclusions, regardless of personal preference or prior commitment.

1c. Fairness

Applies impartial balance between (a) empathy (context, constraints, intent) and (b) accountability norms (reciprocity, consequences, repair) in reciprocal interactions, irrespective of status, convenience, mood, or sense of moral position.

Example: Admits mistakes to prevent double standards and avoid hypocrisy.

1d. Self-Reflection

Analyzes past outcomes by accurately attributing results to internal choices and external factors, focusing on process and controllable elements to (a) detect recurring patterns, (b) identify genuine levers of influence, and (c) convert insight into implementation rules, avoiding rumination by translating reflection into testable action.

1e. Self-Efficacy

Maintains confidence aligned with evidence by (a) grounding self-belief firmly in verified past competencies and achievements in demonstrated competencies, (b) viewing setbacks as skill gaps rather than personal deficits, and (c) regularly re-testing abilities to confirm current capability and prevent skill atrophy.

Examples: (a) integrates achievements into self-concept, (b) treats setbacks as training data.

2. Self-Discipline

Applies self-regulation consistently to align behavior with long-term goals and principles by (a) choosing priorities, (b) sustaining execution, and (c) preventing drift into rigidity or complacency.

2a. Temperance

Manages willpower as a finite resource by (a) prioritizing demands according to current importance rather than past investment, (b) minimizing unnecessary decision load and exposure to temptations, and (c) selecting regulation strategies that conserve capacity for highest-priority goals.

Example: Compresses emotions into a single label to regain focus.

2b. Perseverance

Sustains effort amid hardship by (a) countering avoidance impulses, (b) reaffirming commitment to the desired goal, and (c) using exposure and skill-building to reduce fear-driven withdrawal over time.

Examples: (a) treats fear responses as trainable signals, (b) uses graduated exposure to build resilience.
